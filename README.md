# Blinkit Grocery Sales EDA

Exploratory Data Analysis on Blinkit (quick-commerce grocery) sales dataset using Python.

## Objective

Uncover sales patterns, customer preferences (Low Fat vs Regular), category performance, outlet efficiency, and visibility impact to support business decisions.

## Dataset

- **File**: BlinkIT Grocery Data.xlsx  
- **Rows**: 8,523  
- **Main Columns**:  
  Item Type, Fat Content, Weight, Visibility, Sales, Rating  
  Outlet Year, Identifier, Tier (1–3), Size, Type

## Tools

- Python   
- Pandas  
- Matplotlib + Seaborn  
- Jupyter Notebook

## Key Findings

- **Low Fat** items generate ~65% of total sales (1.83× more than Regular)  
- **Top categories**: Fruits & Vegetables, Snack Foods lead significantly  
- No strong correlation between item visibility and sales  
- Ratings consistently high (3.88–4.02) across categories  
- **Tier 3** outlets (especially Medium size) and **Supermarket Type1 in Tier 2** perform best  
- Newer outlets (post-2016) generally outperform older ones

## Business Takeaways

- Prioritize **Low Fat** products and **top categories** (Fruits & Veg, Snacks)  
- Focus expansion on **Tier 3** Medium-sized Supermarket Type1  
- Improve visibility for underperforming categories  
- Modernize older outlets
